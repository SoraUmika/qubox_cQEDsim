Alexandre Blais, Arne L. Grimsmo, S. M. Girvin, and Andreas Wallraff, "Circuit quantum electrodynamics," Reviews of Modern Physics 93, 025005 (2021). DOI: 10.1103/RevModPhys.93.025005

## Summary

- This is the modern review reference for dispersive Hamiltonians, readout conventions, number splitting, and effective nonlinear interactions in circuit QED.
- It summarizes the sign and interpretation of dispersive shifts, the physics of pointer-state readout, and the effective Kerr and cross-Kerr interactions inherited from weak anharmonicity.
- It is the best single review-level source for tutorial pages that explain standard cQED phenomena without reproducing one specific paper figure.

## Relevance to `cqed_sim`

- It should be the default review citation for cross-Kerr, dispersive-shift, number-splitting, and readout-resonator tutorial pages.
- It provides an external anchor for the repo's documented sign conventions around dispersive interactions and readout response.
- It complements the original Blais et al. 2004 architecture paper by covering the mature circuit-QED picture now used throughout the public docs.

## Notes for This Feature Pass

- This review is used here as a conventions and tutorial-grounding reference, not as a single-figure reproduction target.
- When a tutorial makes a standard circuit-QED claim but is not tied to one narrow literature benchmark, this review is the preferred citation.
