G. Kirchmair, B. Vlastakis, Z. Leghtas, S. E. Nigg, H. Paik, E. Ginossar, M. Mirrahimi, L. Frunzio, S. M. Girvin, and R. J. Schoelkopf, "Observation of quantum state collapse and revival due to the single-photon Kerr effect," Nature 495, 205-209 (2013). DOI: 10.1038/nature11902

## Summary

- The paper demonstrates the single-photon Kerr regime in a superconducting cavity and directly observes collapse, cat-state formation, and revival in phase space.
- Its most tutorial-relevant qualitative signature is the progression from an initial coherent blob to a cat-like interference pattern and then back to revival.
- The work provides the canonical cQED reference for Kerr-generated nonclassical state formation in storage resonators.

## Relevance to `cqed_sim`

- It is the natural literature anchor for the Kerr free-evolution tutorial.
- It supports the qualitative interpretation of the Wigner snapshots at quarter, half, and full Kerr periods.
- It also helps distinguish the physically important single-photon Kerr regime from a generic nonlinear-oscillator toy model.

## Notes for This Feature Pass

- The tutorial does not reproduce the experimental dataset; it uses the paper to justify the expected collapse-and-revival phenomenology and cat-state interpretation.
- The repo validation target remains internal consistency with the Kerr Hamiltonian and the expected revival times, not an experimental calibration match.
