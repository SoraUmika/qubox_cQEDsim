F. Beaudoin, M. P. da Silva, Z. Dutton, and A. Blais, "First-order sidebands in circuit QED using qubit frequency modulation," Physical Review A 86, 022305 (2012). DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevA.86.022305

## Summary

- The paper analyzes first-order red and blue sideband transitions in circuit QED generated by modulating the qubit transition frequency.
- Its central practical result is that the modulation activates sideband interactions when the modulation frequency matches the appropriate qubit-cavity detuning condition.
- The paper provides a direct circuit-QED theory reference for sideband resonance conditions beyond generic Floquet textbook discussions.

## Relevance to `cqed_sim`

- It is the most directly relevant theoretical citation for validating red-sideband resonance conditions in the Floquet module's cQED workflows.
- Even though the repository currently exposes an effective structured sideband-drive abstraction rather than a full microscopic flux-modulation model, the same resonance condition is the physically relevant validation target.
- The paper complements the more general Silveri et al. review by providing a circuit-QED-specific sideband reference.

## Notes for This Feature Pass

- The repository validation for this pass checks that the Floquet solution shows maximal hybridization of the effective red-sideband pair at the predicted sideband transition frequency.
- This should be understood as an effective-Hamiltonian validation of the resonance condition, not a full reproduction of the microscopic modulation Hamiltonian treated in the paper.
- Strand et al., Phys. Rev. B 87, 220505(R) (2013), DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevB.87.220505, is the related experimental confirmation of the same first-order sideband mechanism in flux-driven asymmetric transmons.
